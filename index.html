The concept

Three days hiking, two days cycling

The Kumano Kodo is the anchor — three days on an ancient UNESCO pilgrimage trail through cedar forest and mountain villages, ending at Japan's most sacred shrine. The cycling leg is the choice to make.

Kumano Kodo bookings: Use kumano-travel.com — the official Tanabe City Tourism Bureau system. Luggage forwarding is available throughout (bags travel between guesthouses while you hike). Note the booking system runs on Japanese business hours: roughly 2–10am UK time, 6pm–2am PST. Book 3–6 months ahead for spring/autumn.
River boat (Option A): The Kumanogawa pilgrim boat sells out months in advance. If doing Option A, book the boat the moment you book everything else. If it's unavailable, there's a bus alternative but it's not the same experience.
Biwaichi bike rental: Hire from stations near Otsu, Hikone or Maibara. E-bikes are available and recommended for the group — ~¥4,000–8,000/day. Reserve online in advance, especially for weekends and peak season. Drop-off at a different station is usually possible but must be arranged ahead.

Timing

When to go

Spring and autumn are the clear windows. The Kii Peninsula is one of the wettest areas in mainland Japan year-round, but those two seasons offer the most reliable hiking conditions and the best scenery. Summer is genuinely miserable for hiking — hot, humid, and typhoon season overlaps.

🌸
Spring
Late March – May
Cherry blossoms in late March/April. Mild temperatures (10–18°C), generally dry. The most popular window.
  • Cherry blossoms on trail
  • Cool, comfortable hiking
  • Long daylight hours by May
  • Golden Week (late Apr–early May): crowded, accommodation books out
  • Book Kumano guesthouses 6–9 months ahead
🍁
Autumn
October – November
Peak foliage colours in October/November. Crisp air, low humidity, spectacular forest colours along the Kodo.
  • Stunning autumn foliage
  • Coolest, clearest cycling
  • Less crowded than spring
  • Early September: typhoon risk
  • Late November: days shorten
  • Peak foliage weeks book fast
☀️
Summer
June – August
Rainy season in June. July–August: 25–35°C with high humidity on the trail. Typhoon risk from August.
  • Oppressive heat and humidity
  • Typhoon disruption risk
  • Kumano is very wet in June
❄️
Winter
December – February
Quiet trails, 3–10°C. Small chance of snow. Onsen are especially good. Shorter daylight hours.
  • Very few other hikers
  • Onsen at their best
  • Short days, early dark
  • Some guesthouses close

Sweet spot: October is the single best month — stable weather, autumn foliage beginning, not yet the November rush. Mid-October is ideal for both hiking and Biwaichi cycling. Late April (avoiding Golden Week itself, which ends 5 May) is the spring equivalent.

Getting there

Flights into Osaka (KIX)

Both departure cities connect well to Kansai International. The SF Bay Area actually has better direct options than London — the key is coordinating arrival times so the group can travel to Tanabe together on Day 1.

London (LHR)
No direct service. Best connections: Finnair via Helsinki, KLM via Amsterdam, Qatar via Doha, Lufthansa via Frankfurt. All ~14–16 hrs total.

San Francisco (SFO)
Daily nonstop with United and ANA. ~11h 40m direct to KIX. JAL also serves this route. Best-value month is typically September.

Coordination tip: Align arrivals at KIX on the same evening where possible. From KIX to Kii-Tanabe takes about 2 hours via the JR Haruka to Osaka, then the limited express Kuroshio. If the group can arrive by early afternoon, you can reach Tanabe before dark and be ready for Day 1 proper the next morning.
Japan Rail Pass: Worth buying for this trip if you're doing the Kumano Kodo + cycling combo — the Shinkansen connections and limited express trains add up. Purchase before leaving home (required); validate on arrival. The 7-day pass (~¥50,000) covers most of your rail needs. Note: the Kumano Travel booking window runs on Japanese hours — roughly 2–10am UK / 6pm–2am PST.

Logistics

Tips & things to sort early

🏔️
Book the Kumano Kodo early
The guesthouses along the Nakahechi route are small and limited. Book 6–9 months ahead for spring or autumn. The Kumano Travel system handles accommodation + luggage forwarding. The booking window is Japanese business hours: roughly 09:00–18:00 JST (01:00–10:00 UK / 17:00–02:00 PST the day before).
🚤
The river boat sells out fast
If doing Option A, the Kumanogawa pilgrim boat from Hongu to Shingu is one of the highlights — but it books out months ahead. Reserve it at the same time as everything else, or have a bus backup plan ready.
🚲
Reserve e-bikes for Biwaichi
E-bikes (electric-assist) are the right call for a mixed-fitness group on the lake loop — they make the 50 km days comfortable and remove pressure from slower riders. Reserve well ahead, especially for weekends. Rental stations near Otsu, Hikone, and Maibara Station. Bike tolls are currently waived on Biwaichi. For Shimanami, e-bike hire starts at ¥8,000/day from Onomichi.
🎒
Luggage forwarding (takuhaibin)
On the Kumano Kodo, your main bags are forwarded between guesthouses daily so you only carry a daypack. On the cycling days, use Sagawa Express or Yamato Transport to send bags between hotels. Most Japanese convenience stores (7-Eleven, FamilyMart) accept parcels for next-day delivery.
💴
Cash still matters
Many guesthouses and rural stops on both routes are cash-only. Keep at least ¥20,000–30,000 per person in cash at all times. 7-Eleven ATMs reliably accept international cards. Card acceptance is improving but don't count on it for small inns or trail-side cafes.
🌧️
Expect some rain
The Kii Peninsula is one of the wettest regions in Japan year-round. Pack proper waterproof gear, not just a packable shell. Good waterproof hiking boots matter on the stone paths. On the Kodo, rain makes the forest atmosphere genuinely otherworldly — embrace it.
🥢
Meals at guesthouses
Most Kumano guesthouses offer dinner and breakfast as part of the rate (half-board). The meals are home-cooked regional Japanese food — one of the best parts of the trail. Declare dietary restrictions when booking. Vegetarian is usually accommodated; other restrictions may be harder. Confirm in writing.
📱
Data and navigation
Get a Japan data SIM or eSIM on arrival (or pre-purchase online). Google Maps works well for both routes. For Biwaichi, download the free "BIWAICHI Cycling Navi" app — it has GPS routing and English support. On the Kodo, the trail is well-marked with wooden signs, but Google Maps is useful as backup.
♨️
Onsen etiquette
Most guesthouses on the Kodo have communal onsen (hot spring baths). These are taken without swimwear. Tattoos may be prohibited at some establishments — if this applies to anyone in the group, ask when booking. Yunomine Onsen in particular is one of the oldest hot springs in Japan and is genuinely special after a day of hiking.
